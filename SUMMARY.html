Final architecture

Tokenizer
  • Byte-level BPE, vocab 4096, trained on the corpus only
  • Lossless byte fallback (round-trip verified)
  • ~4.0 bytes/token vs 1.0 for the byte baseline
  • Incremental lazy-heap trainer: full 7 MB in ~8 s
Model & training
  • GPT, 4 layers, n_embd 160, 4 heads, block 256
  • Tied input/output embeddings (fits the 2M cap)
  • RoPE positions, RMSNorm, GELU MLP
  • AdamW, warmup+cosine to 10%, wd 0.1, clip 1.0
  • batch 32, peak LR 2e-3, 2000 steps, CPU

What was tried and rejected (and why)

Every fashionable modern-arch trick that failed failed for the same reason — it is tuned for long, stable training this 2000-step cap does not provide: GPT-2 small-init + residual scaling (underfits in 2000 steps), QK-Norm (discards q/k magnitude the small model uses; also does not unlock a higher LR), squared-ReLU (helped at block128, hurt at block256 — noise). The tricks that won either change the data representation (BPE) or add a sound, parameter-free prior (RoPE).
